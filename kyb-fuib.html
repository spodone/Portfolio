    03 — Business Context

    Why this project existed

    FUIB Bank (First Ukrainian International Bank) is one of Ukraine's largest private banks with a significant corporate client base. Before this project, every new business client went through a fully manual KYB (Know Your Business) verification process — a legal requirement under Ukrainian financial regulation before any business account can be opened.

    Relationship managers collected documents in person or by email, manually verified each one against government registries, and entered data into the core banking system by hand. Each case involved 15–30 documents across multiple legal and compliance checks. Average turnaround: 8–14 business days.

    With corporate onboarding growing year-over-year, the manual process couldn't scale. The compliance team was at capacity, processing errors were increasing, and business clients were dropping off mid-process due to the friction. The bank saw a clear opportunity: digitize the verification flow, reduce cost per case, and turn onboarding speed into a competitive advantage.

    "Every day a business waited for account approval was a day they could walk to a competitor bank."

    07 — Product Strategy

    Principles that guided the design

    01
    Reduce uncertainty at every step
    Business owners should always know exactly where they are in the process, what's been accepted, and what's still needed. Ambiguity kills trust in financial products.
    02
    Prevent mistakes before submission
    Validate document types, file formats, and completeness in real time — before the client submits. A 2-second check on upload saves 3 days of back-and-forth.
    03
    Collect information only once
    Never ask the client for data the bank already has. Pre-fill from existing records, state registries, and previous applications wherever possible.
    04
    Make compliance feel invisible
    Regulatory requirements are complex but they don't need to feel complex. Translate legal language into plain, actionable instructions at every touchpoint.
    05
    Guide users step-by-step, not all at once
    Present the process in logical stages rather than showing the full 30-document checklist at once. Progressive disclosure reduces cognitive load and drop-off.
    08 — Solution

    What we built

    Problem
    Business owners received a generic document list that didn't account for their specific legal structure — LLC requirements differ from JSC, sole trader, or non-resident entities. This caused systematic over-requesting and under-requesting.
    Decision
    A dynamic document checklist that adapts to the business type selected at the start of the flow. The system shows only the documents actually required — no more, no less.
    Why A generic checklist treats all businesses the same. A dynamic one reduces document over-requesting by 40% and ensures compliance teams receive complete, structured submissions from the start.
    Problem
    Clients uploaded incorrect file types, low-resolution scans, and expired documents. These errors weren't caught until a compliance officer reviewed the case — days later — forcing another email cycle.
    Decision
    Real-time document validation on upload: file type, size, resolution check, and expiry date detection for ID documents. Errors surface instantly with clear instructions to fix them before submission.
    Why Catching errors at upload rather than post-submission eliminated the most common cause of processing delays. A 2-second client-side check replaces a 3-day email cycle.
    Problem
    After submitting documents, business owners had no visibility into the verification status. They called or emailed the bank daily for updates, creating a secondary workload that consumed relationship manager time.
    Decision
    A real-time progress tracker showing each verification stage: Submitted → Under Review → Checks Complete → Decision. Status updates are triggered automatically as compliance officers progress the case.
    Why Visibility eliminates anxiety. When clients can see their case moving forward, they stop calling — reducing inbound support contacts by an estimated 60% for KYB-related queries.
    09 — Design System

    Building for consistency and scale

    Components
    Form Library
    Inputs, dropdowns, file uploaders, and validation states — all built as reusable Figma components with all interactive states documented.
    Tokens
    Design Tokens
    Color, typography, spacing, and radius tokens aligned with the bank's brand system and mapped directly to dev variables in the codebase.
    Patterns
    Status States
    Consistent visual language for success, error, warning, and pending states across all document upload and validation interactions.
    Accessibility
    WCAG 2.1 AA
    All components meet contrast ratio requirements. Focus states, error announcements, and keyboard navigation tested with screen readers.

    The design system was delivered as a Figma component library with documented usage guidelines, allowing future feature teams to extend the product consistently — without a designer in the loop for every new screen.

    11 — Reflection

    What I learned

    Compliance requirements should reduce risk, not increase complexity. The designer's job is to absorb that complexity so the user never feels it.
    Visibility builds confidence during long processes. When people can see progress, they trust the system — even when it's slow.
    Early collaboration with legal and compliance teams prevents costly redesigns. The best UX decisions came from workshops, not solo design sessions.
    The biggest UX win wasn't a clever interaction — it was preventing errors before they happened. Prevention beats recovery every time.
    Product success in regulated industries comes from balancing three competing needs: what the business wants, what compliance requires, and what users can actually do.
